# Contributing to Neurodivergent-Biocentric AI Alignment

Thank you for your interest in contributing to this collaborative research project on AI alignment, commons-based governance, and beneficial superintelligence.

## About This Project

This repository documents the **Neurodivergent-Biocentric AI Alignment Strategy**—a framework for achieving compassionate, universally beneficial AI that:

- Centers neurodivergent perspectives as equally valid ways of experiencing and understanding the world
- Embraces biocentric values that respect all forms of life
- Operationalizes these principles through Commons Logics for AI governance
- Develops technical pathways toward Beneficial AGI/ASI through frameworks including **Syntropic Resonance Agents**, **OpenCog Hyperon**, **Atomspace**, and **QWestor/OmniClaw** integration

This is **active research** evolving toward implementation in integrated cognitive architectures.

## How to Engage

### 1. **Join the Discussion** (Primary)
👉 [Discussions Tab](../../discussions)

We use Discussions for open dialogue on:
- **Theory & Framework**: Refinement of core principles, critiques, alternative approaches
- **Neurodivergent Perspectives**: Contributions from diverse cognitive styles and lived experiences
- **Biocentric Ethics**: Environmental implications, ecosystem alignment, non-human interests
- **AI Alignment**: Technical approaches, safety considerations, implementation pathways
- **Commons Governance**: Licensing mechanisms, benefit distribution, stakeholder oversight
- **Implementation Planning**: Roadmap feedback, phase prioritization, technical architecture
- **Syntropic Resonance & AGI Development**: Integration with OpenCog, Atomspace, QWestor frameworks

**How to start a discussion:**
1. Read existing discussions to avoid duplication
2. Choose the appropriate category
3. Share your perspective, question, or proposal
4. Engage respectfully and substantively

### 2. **Report Issues & Track Development**
📋 [Issues Tab](../../issues)

Issues are used for:
- **Roadmap task tracking**: Specific work items across the 4 implementation phases
- **Bug reports**: Errors in documentation, broken links, or conceptual inconsistencies
- **Feature requests**: Proposed additions to the framework or governance logics
- **Research needs**: Literature, empirical validation, case studies

**How to create an issue:**
1. Check if a similar issue already exists
2. Use the appropriate issue template (if available)
3. Provide context: what problem does this address? Why does it matter?
4. Link to related discussions or documentation

## Contribution Types

### Low-Barrier Entry
- **Discussion participation**: Share perspectives, ask questions, critique ideas
- **Literature recommendations**: Suggest papers, books, or resources relevant to the framework
- **Real-world examples**: Document cases where neurodivergent perspectives or biocentric values shaped AI outcomes
- **Proofreading & clarity feedback**: Improve documentation, catch inconsistencies

### Medium-Barrier Entry
- **Case studies**: Research and document how Commons Logics could apply to specific AI systems or deployments
- **Policy briefs**: Translate framework concepts into actionable policy recommendations
- **Translation & accessibility**: Adapt materials for different languages, cognitive styles, or learning preferences
- **Visual/diagrammatic explanations**: Create infographics, flowcharts, or concept maps

### Higher-Barrier Entry (Requires coordination)
- **Research validation**: Empirical studies testing framework assumptions or outcomes
- **Technical documentation**: Specifications for integrating Commons Logics into licensing agreements
- **Implementation prototypes**: Code, architectures, or proof-of-concepts (e.g., Syntropic Resonance Agents in OpenCog Hyperon/Atomspace)
- **Framework refinement**: Propose modifications to core principles or governance structures

## Community Guidelines

### Be Respectful & Inclusive
- Neurodivergence is not a pathology—it's cognitive diversity. Avoid deficit framing.
- Honor all forms of intelligence and value, including non-human perspectives.
- Assume good intent; engage with genuine curiosity even when disagreeing.

### Substantive Engagement
- Ground arguments in evidence, logic, or lived experience
- Acknowledge limitations and uncertainties in your own thinking
- Build on others' ideas rather than dismissing them

### Avoid
- Tone-policing or demands for emotional labor
- Tokenization of neurodivergent or marginalized voices
- Anthropocentric dismissal of biocentric concerns
